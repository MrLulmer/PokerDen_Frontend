Texas Hold'em

History

The history of poker dates back to the late 18th century when the first game similar to poker was played by French colonialists in New Orleans. The game spread up the Mississippi River and out west during the Gold Rush. During the Civil War, it evolved with many additions. It gained a reputation as a "sinner’s game," later fueled by Hollywood’s Wild West depictions. Texas Hold’em, created in Robstown, Texas, during the early 1900s, remained obscure until its Las Vegas launch in the 1970s.

The Basics

Texas Hold’em is played with five community cards and two private cards (hole cards). Players aim to make the best possible five-card hand using these cards.

Gameplay

Blinds

Markers indicate the dealer, small blind, and big blind. The small blind must post a predetermined amount, and the big blind posts double. The dealer then deals two cards to each player, starting with the small blind.

Betting Options

  • Bet: Start the betting by placing chips into the pot.
  • Call: Match the amount bet by the previous player.
  • Fold: Discard your hand and exit the round.
  • Raise: Increase the current bet.
  • Check: Stay in the hand without betting (only if no prior bet has been made).

Pre-Flop

After receiving hole cards, players can call the big blind, raise, or fold. Starting left of the big blind, no checks are allowed unless you are the big blind.

The Flop

The dealer reveals three community cards. Betting starts to the left of the dealer button. If no bets have been made, players can check.

The Turn

The dealer reveals a fourth community card. Betting proceeds as in the flop.

The River

The dealer reveals the fifth and final community card. Betting proceeds as before.

Showdown

Remaining players reveal their cards. If no bets occurred in the final round, the player left of the button shows their cards first. The best five-card hand wins the pot.
